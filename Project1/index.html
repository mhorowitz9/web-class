Virgin River

~Robyn Carr

Book 1 of the Virgin River Series

Website Design Project 1: By Miri Horowitz

After the tragic death of her husband, Mel Monroe, a nurse and midwife, yearns to begin a fresh start to escape her grief. Mel immediately accepts a job to practice her profession in a small rural town far away from her city life by seeking to replace her once hectic city life with one of privacy and serenity. Despite Virgin River's small population, her life still includes unexpected hurdles, and she could never picture herself falling in love with the restaurateur and former marine Jack.

Characters

Mel Monroe

Mel is the main character, and she is a nurse and midwife. While Mel is friendly and caring, she holds back on revealing her past to the people of Virgin River. Throughout the book, Mel vents about her hardships to her only family, her sister, Joey, who lives back in the city. When Mel meets Jack, she tries to resist her feelings towards him due to the tragic death of her husband, ER doctor, Mark, in a robbery a few months ago. As Mel gets to know Jack, she eventually opens up to him, and their relationship grows stronger.

Jack Sheridan

Jack is a resident of Virgin River, and he has the most popular restaurant in town. When Mel meets him at his bar, he immediately becomes interested in Mel, especially the mystery behind her past. While Mel struggles with grief, Jack faces the trauma of PTSD due to losing a soldier in combat when he was a marine. Throughout the book, Jack and Mel help each other with their hardships.

Vernon Mullins(aka Doc)

Doctor Mullins is the only doctor located in the town of Virgin River. He is an older man grappling with the thought of retiring due to his declining physical health. At first, he is strict and tough on Mel because he isn't used to being assisted. As Mel and him get to know each other, he begins to use their area of expertise in the medical field to treat more patients at a time.

Hope McCrea

Hope is the mayor of Virgin River who hired Mel to assist Doc. When Mel is urged to leave Virgin River because of the complex adjustments to the rural town, she encourages her to stay.

Joey Barnes

Joey is Mel's older sister, married with kids, and lives in the city. She is very protective of Mel's impulsive decision-making. Although she tries to convince Mel to return to the town, she changes her mind and encourages Mel to stay when she notices her sister's happiness increasing.

Lessons

Change in Location, Change of Heart

Mel decides to move Virgin River despite not knowing anyone or visiting it. The move helps her cope with her grief, and she meets her love, Jack.

Compatibility in the Unexpected

Mel and Jack come from opposite backgrounds: a rural boy and a city girl. They have entirely different careers, too. However, their personalities go well together because they are both caring people and always listen to one another.

Embrace Community

Virgin River is a small town; therefore, a limited group of people can do specific jobs. As a result, for better functioning of the town, people step up by helping each other when they can. For example, Jack's restaurant is one of the main ones in Virgin River, and he offers excellent prices instead of increasing them to allow people to afford high-quality food. On the internal level, the community displays kindness and compassion towards one another by welcoming Mel despite her being from a big city.
