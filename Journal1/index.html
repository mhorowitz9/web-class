Journal 1

~Miri Horowitz

Think of a website as a single book in a library.


Each book has a different purpose, whether that be exploring genres or just a cookbook.
The covers are uniquely designed; some may be plain, while others are decorative.
Within books, there are chapters dividing that book into manageable sections.
While reading the book, a person can reread chapters and skip other sections.
You can even share that book with other people to read it.
When you are finished reading, you might even be more knowledgeable about certain topics.

That is exactly what a website is: a digital library!


Filled with any books you want at any moment!
Websites can look wildly different from one another; once you go into that website, you can explore certain sections with the navigation bars.
Most importantly, every website has individual goals, one of the main reasons people display things online. From promoting a business to even reading a blog, there are endless possibilities.

Websites will become the new museums.

They will consist of artifacts and news articles from many years ago. Websites are the source of information, therefore they will not become obsolete.
Websites are the new platform for communication between people, and it allows information to flow.
